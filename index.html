About

From a decade in banking in Lima to a PhD in Nutritional Sciences at the University of Toronto, I've built a career at the intersection of research and policy.

I use simulation modeling and data analysis to help governments and international organizations implement evidence-based food and nutrition policies — from front-of-package labeling to sodium reduction targets.

My work with PAHO/WHO and the Global Health Advocacy Incubator has supported policy advocacy campaigns across Latin America, Asia, and Africa, translating complex research into actionable policy recommendations.

Policy Analysis & Modeling

Simulation models assessing dietary and health impacts of food policy interventions, including front-of-package labeling and sodium reduction strategies.

Research Protocol Development

Standardized procedures for evaluating nutritional quality of processed foods using nutrient profiling models across national food supplies.

Technical Assistance

Direct support to governments and organizations on adopting and implementing mandatory nutrition policies aligned with international targets.

Capacity Building

Training workshops and research-for-advocacy sessions for partners across 25+ countries on food policy best practices.

Global Reach

Technical assistance, research, and policy advocacy across 27 countries and 4 continents.
